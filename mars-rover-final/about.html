Frequently Asked Questions

These quick answers cover why NASA sends rovers to Mars, how they work, and what we are learning from their missions on the Red Planet.

Why send rovers to Mars?

To study Mars’s environment and search for clues about ancient life.

How do rovers communicate?

They send data to Mars orbiters, which relay it back to Earth.

How long do missions last?

Most rovers last years longer than their planned missions.

What have they discovered?

Evidence of ancient lakes, rivers, and water-altered minerals.

Where can I learn more?

Visit NASA’s official Mars Exploration Program: mars.nasa.gov
