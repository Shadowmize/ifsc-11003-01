Mission History

Each rover mission built on the discoveries of the one before it, moving from first looks at Martian rocks to detailed searches for signs of ancient life.

Spirit & Opportunity

Launch: 2003

Landing: January 2004

Spirit and Opportunity greatly exceeded their 90-day missions, exploring Mars for years and finding strong evidence of past water.

Curiosity

Launch: November 2011

Landing: August 2012

Curiosity explores Gale Crater, studying rock layers that reveal how Mars changed across billions of years.

Perseverance

Launch: July 2020

Landing: February 2021

Perseverance landed in Jezero Crater, searching for signs of ancient life and collecting samples for possible return to Earth.
