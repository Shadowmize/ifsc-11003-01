Frequently Asked Questions

Curious about Mars and its robotic explorers? These questions and answers cover the basics of the Mars Rover Program.

Why does NASA send rovers to Mars?

NASA sends rovers to Mars to learn more about the planet’s history, geology, climate, and potential for life. Rovers can move across the surface, perform experiments, and send detailed data and images back to Earth.

How do rovers communicate with Earth?

Rovers usually send data to orbiters around Mars first. Those orbiters then relay the information back to large radio antennas on Earth. This process allows scientists and engineers to receive data and send new commands.

How long do Mars rover missions last?

Each mission has a planned length, but many rovers operate much longer than expected. For example, Opportunity was planned for about 90 days but continued exploring for nearly 15 years.

What are some key discoveries from the rovers?

Mars rovers have found rocks and minerals that form in water, evidence of ancient streambeds and lakes, and signs that Mars once had conditions that could have supported microbial life.
