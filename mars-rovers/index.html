Welcome to the Mars Rover Program

NASA’s Mars Rover Program sends robotic explorers to the surface of Mars to study its rocks, soil, atmosphere, and climate. These rovers are mobile science labs that help scientists investigate whether Mars could have once supported life.

Over the years, rovers such as Spirit, Opportunity, Curiosity, and Perseverance have traveled across the Martian surface, capturing images, drilling into rocks, and sending data back to Earth.

To learn more straight from NASA, visit the official Mars Exploration Program website.

Featured Mars Rovers

Curiosity

Curiosity landed on Mars in 2012. Its main mission is to determine if Mars ever had the right environmental conditions to support microbial life. It studies rocks and soil, and analyzes the chemistry of the Martian surface.

Perseverance

Perseverance landed in 2021 inside Jezero Crater. It searches for signs of ancient life and collects rock samples that could be brought back to Earth in future missions. It also tests new technologies for future human exploration.

Spirit & Opportunity

The twin rovers Spirit and Opportunity landed on Mars in 2004. They were originally planned for 90-day missions but lasted for years. They helped prove that liquid water once flowed on the surface of Mars.
